Here are some healthy practices that we recommend you to follow!

For Crops

Crop Rotation: Rotate crops to prevent soil depletion and maintain soil fertility. Different crops have different nutrient needs, and rotating them can help balance soil nutrients.

Cover Cropping: Plant cover crops during off-seasons to protect the soil from erosion, improve soil health, and suppress weeds.

Water Management: Implement water-saving irrigation techniques such as drip irrigation and rainwater harvesting to conserve water and reduce water wastage.

For Cattle

Proper Nutrition: Provide a balanced diet rich in nutrients, minerals, and vitamins to ensure the health and productivity of cattle. Consult with a veterinarian or animal nutritionist for guidance.

Regular Health Check-ups: Schedule regular check-ups with a veterinarian to monitor the health of cattle, vaccinate against diseases, and treat any health issues promptly.

Avoid Overcrowding: Ensure that cattle have enough space to move around freely and avoid overcrowding in sheds or pastures, as this can lead to stress and disease spread.
